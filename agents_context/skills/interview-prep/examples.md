# Interview Prep — Examples

## Example STAR Talking Point
**Question:** "Tell me about a time you had to push back on a deadline."

**Resume evidence:** "Led migration of billing service to a new provider
under a Q3 deadline; identified a data-integrity risk and negotiated a
2-week extension with stakeholders."

**Talking point:**
> Situation: I was leading a billing-provider migration with a hard Q3
> deadline. Task: partway through, I found a data-integrity risk in the
> migration plan. Action: I raised it immediately to my manager and the
> stakeholders with a concrete remediation plan and asked for a 2-week
> extension rather than shipping a risky migration. Result: we got the
> extension, shipped with zero billing incidents, and the extension request
> became the template for how the team handled similar tradeoffs afterward.

## Example — No Strong Match
If the job asks about "managing a team of 10+" and the resume shows only
individual-contributor work: don't invent a management story. Instead:
> This resume doesn't show direct people-management experience. The closest
> available example is leading a 3-person project team on the billing
> migration above — worth being upfront in the interview about growth into
> people management rather than overstating past scope.

## Example — Company-Specific Question to Ask

> that changed the roadmap priorities for this team over the next couple of
> quarters?"
