# Interview Prep — Detailed Instructions

## The STAR Method
Every behavioral talking point should be structured as:
- **Situation** — brief context (team, project, constraint).
- **Task** — what needed to happen and why it mattered.
- **Action** — what the candidate specifically did (not "we" — "I").
- **Result** — a quantified or concrete outcome.

## Question Categories
Generate a balanced mix:
1. **Behavioral** (4-6): leadership, conflict, failure, ambiguity, ownership.
2. **Technical / role-specific** (4-6): pulled from the job description's
   required skills and responsibilities.
3. **Company-specific** (2-3): based on company research — recent product
   launches, strategy, or challenges the company is publicly facing.

## Mapping Questions to Resume Evidence
For each behavioral question, scan the resume for the closest matching real
example. If no strong match exists, say so rather than inventing one — offer
the closest available example and note it's an imperfect fit.

## Questions to Ask the Interviewer
Favor questions that:
- Reference something specific from company research (a recent launch, a
  stated priority, team structure).
- Show genuine interest in how the role/team operates, not compensation or
  benefits (save those for a recruiter conversation).
